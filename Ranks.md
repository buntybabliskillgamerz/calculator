## Rank Structure and Authority

The Yumic Organization operates under a tiered rank framework designed to distribute responsibility without centralizing identity.

Ranks exist to define **scope of authority**, **access boundaries**, and **documentation responsibility**. They do not represent status, entitlement, or permanence.

All ranks are subject to review.

---

### Foundational Leadership

The founding individuals of the Yumic Organization are not publicly identified.

Records referencing early organizational formation indicate the presence of multiple founding contributors operating under shared authority. Personal identifiers were intentionally excluded from public documentation.

Foundational leadership is referenced collectively in archival material. No singular figure is attributed with unilateral control.

This anonymity is maintained to preserve structural continuity and prevent dependency on individual identity.

---

### Executive Oversight

Executive-level authority exists to maintain organizational alignment across divisions.

Individuals operating at this level:
- Do not appear in public-facing records
- Are referenced only through role designation
- Operate through delegated authority rather than direct intervention

Executive oversight focuses on systemic stability rather than operational detail.

---

## Rank Classifications

### Observer

**Authority Scope:** Minimal  
**Function:** Documentation exposure and monitored interaction

Observers possess no decision-making authority. Their actions are recorded solely for evaluation and archival reference.

---

### Associate

**Authority Scope:** Limited  
**Function:** Assigned task participation under supervision

Associates operate within clearly defined boundaries. Access is restricted to non-sensitive systems.

---

### Operative

**Authority Scope:** Moderate  
**Function:** Execution of defined operations

Operatives are authorized to act within approved parameters. Autonomy is conditional and reviewed regularly.

---

### Analyst

**Authority Scope:** Moderate  
**Function:** Evaluation, interpretation, and reporting

Analysts influence outcomes indirectly through documentation and assessment rather than direct action.

---

### Coordinator

**Authority Scope:** Elevated  
**Function:** Oversight of limited personnel or processes

Coordinators facilitate alignment between roles and ensure procedural compliance.

---

### Director

**Authority Scope:** High  
**Function:** Divisional oversight and strategic implementation

Directors are responsible for long-term consistency within assigned divisions. Authority is contextual and non-transferable.

---

### Executive Authority

**Authority Scope:** Systemic  
**Function:** Organizational continuity and policy alignment

This rank does not engage in direct operations. Decisions are recorded, abstracted, and implemented through lower tiers.

Identity at this level is not documented in public records.

---

## Authority Limitations

Authority within the Yumic Organization is:
- Role-bound
- Contextual
- Revocable
- Documented

No rank confers permanent control.

---

## Anonymity Protocol

Personal identities associated with:
- Founders
- Executive Authority
- Cross-divisional oversight

are intentionally excluded from public-facing documentation.

Archival references prioritize role function over individual attribution.

This protocol is permanent.
